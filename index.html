New Hampshire residences wasted the highest quantity of food per person in America in 2021

Liz C. Lozano
Dec. 17th, 2023

Produce and dairy and eggs represented 48,7% of the total residential waste. Other states, expected to be the highest, reported a low waste per person, such as California and New York.

With 404 pounds per person per year, this state had the highest food waste rate per person in 2021. The analysis of the data, disclosed recently by ReFED, also showed that of the total of 280.000 tons, 91.900 (29,9%) were produce, followed by dairy and eggs, with 57.600 tons (18,8%).

Also, other states appeared at the top, such as Maine (356), Arizona (338), Florida (328), and Arkansas (326).

In contrast, while California (2,775,000 tons) and New York (1,839,100 tons) showed a high total amount of residential food wasted, its data per person at home was among the lowest in America. California reported only 141,8 pounds per person, and New York wasted 185,2 pounds. One of the lowest in the country. According to Hillary Bosch, Outreach Coordinator of the Bureau of Recycling and Sustainability of Brooklyn in New York City, “organic material comprises about the third of the residential waste stream (…) also, there are some districts that are still working on improving in NYC.”

Data source: ReFED
